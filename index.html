Well completed classes

01005 Advanced Engineering Mathematics 1

The course content is the mathematical basis for a broad range of technical fields and also provides a starting point for further studies in mathematics and applied mathematics. The dominating theme of the course is linearity. The goal is to give students the ability to employ fundamental tools of mathematics in theoretical studies as well as in applied thematic exercises and projects. The use of modern computer software supports both of these aspects.

02462 Signals and Data

This course imparts a thorough understanding of intelligent systems, covering signal and data types, representation of signals (images, sound, video, text), signal processing, and probabilistic methods. Participants learn to use Python for sensing, representing, and analyzing signals. The course facilitates working with signals in real-world AI systems.

02463 Active Machine Learning and Agency

This course focuses on essential human competences such as active learning, agency, and causal reasoning. The primary objective is to introduce active Bayesian machine learning models, emphasizing the limitations of purely observational data and the significance of agency and "embodiment" in machine learning algorithms. The curriculum provides an introductory exploration of the theoretical aspects of Bayesian optimization, active learning, and causal inference.

02464 Artificial Intelligence and Human Cognition

In many tasks, Artificial Intelligence (AI) aims to solve problems that humans can solve such as object recognition, classification and motor control. Although the tasks humans and computers solve can seem similar they may differ in terms of goals, training data and available processing power. The underlying computations and algorithms in AI systems might also be very different from those implemented in the human brain. This course presents elements of cognitive science and neuroscience illustrating how the human brain process information and relates this to information processing in AI systems.

02809 User Experience Design

Upon course completion, students will master identifying user needs, optimizing unique value propositions, modeling user needs hierarchically, prioritizing UX prototype designs, modeling microinteractions, implementing backend interactions, selecting design patterns, designing minimum viable products, validating MVPs, and describing methodologies and design principles for digital products. They'll also understand key digital product development processes and considerations for transitioning from development to production, including scalability.

PSYC 2: Biological Foundations

This course provides an introductory survey of the relationship between human behavior and brain function. Specific areas of emphasis include vision and other sensory processes, memory, motivation, attention, and cognition.
